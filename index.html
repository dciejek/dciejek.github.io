About Me

Hi! I'm a student who loves the intersection of technology and business. I built this website to introduce myself and highlight a few projects I'm proud of. Recently I've been exploring how data engineering and modern web frameworks can make financial information easier to understand. I enjoy shipping small, useful tools and iterating based on feedback. When I'm not coding, you'll find me reading about markets, practicing thoughtful design, or tinkering with dashboards that turn messy data into clean, visual stories.

My academic work focuses on computer science fundamentals—data structures, systems, and algorithms—paired with finance, accounting, and economics. That mix shows up in my projects: I've built a contract-clause analyzer (FastAPI + React), experimented with an alternative-data investment dashboard, and automated spreadsheet workflows that compute metrics like IRR, NPV, and factor exposures. I care about clarity, reliability, and performance: clear UX so people understand what they're seeing, reliable data pipelines so numbers are reproducible, and performant code so the experience feels fast. This site has two pages: the Home you're reading now and a second page that expands on Berkshire County, where I'm from.
