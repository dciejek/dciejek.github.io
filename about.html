Berkshire County

Attractions

Berkshire County has many attractions including beautiful parks, museums, and cultural events, many of which are season specific. In the summer, you can visit Tanglewood for concerts, explore the Norman Rockwell Museum, or hike in the Mount Greylock State Reservation. During the fall, the foliage is stunning, making it a perfect time for scenic drives and outdoor activities, such as a hike around Benedict Pond or a trip to Bartlett's Apple Orchard. Winter brings opportunities for skiing and snowboarding at resorts like Jiminy Peak, while spring is ideal for enjoying the blooming flowers at places like the Berkshire Botanical Garden. All of these attractions make Berkshire County a year-round destination for visitors, and residents alike.

There is also no shortage of year-round attractions including The Clark Art Institute, Mass MoCA, and the Berkshire Museum, offering a variety of art, history, and culture to explore for all ages. Not to mention the beautiful small towns like Lenox, Stockbridge, and Great Barrington, each with their own unique charm, shops, and dining options. Whether you're a history buff, an art enthusiast, or an outdoor adventurer, Berkshire County has something to offer. Its unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and welcoming community makes it a truly special place to experience.
